First-Class Mail Processing Categories

PRESORTING REQUIREMENTS & DATA QUALIFICATIONS – LETTERS & FLATS

🎯

The Presort Pool: A mailing must contain a minimum of 500 pieces to qualify for presort processing. Once the minimum volume is met, the software evaluates each mailpiece for Automation eligibility based on CASS and NCOA (Move Update) data quality.

SINGLE-PIECE Volume < 500 Pieces
Paid at Full Commercial Rate
PRESORT BY DATA QUALITY & MACHINABILITY LEVEL
AUTOMATION (Highest Discount Tier)
WHAT IT MEANS
✉️
REQUIREMENTS
  • Barcode: 11-Digit IMb Required (Full Delivery Point Routing).
  • Printed Address: 5-Digit or 9-Digit ZIP Code on the mailpiece block.
  • Data: Must pass CASS & NCOA (Move Update) verification.
  • Physical: All pieces must be 100% Machinable.
Sorted into 5-Digit, AADC, or Mixed AADC Trays.
NON-AUTOMATION (Machinable Presort)
WHAT IT MEANS
✉️
REQUIREMENTS
  • Barcode: 5-Digit or 9-Digit IMb (or no barcode).
  • Printed Address: 5-Digit or 9-Digit ZIP Code printed on block.
  • Data: Lacks full 11-Digit assignment but meets Presort minimums.
  • Physical: Must be Machinable.
Receives a lower discount than Automation rates.
RESIDUAL / FALLOUT (Data Failures in Presort Pool)
WHAT IT MEANS
🗂️
REQUIREMENTS
  • Origin: Mailpieces from the 500+ pool that failed CASS/NCOA.
  • Sortation: No minimum volume required. Do NOT mix with Presort trays.
  • Letters Pricing: Flat Rate pricing up to 3.5oz.
  • Flats Pricing: Paid at Commercial Single-Piece exact weight rates.
Must be placed in a separate tray labeled "WKG / RESIDUAL".
TRAY PREPARATION

Use standardized USPS white trays.

AUTO MACH

Never mix Residual mail inside Automation/Presorted trays!

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS & eDoc
  • All mailpiece processing logic is defined by USPS DMM rules.
  • eDoc Submission: Mail.xml must correctly flag Residual pieces to prevent PostalOne! validation errors.
  • The 11-digit barcode is strictly for OCR Scanners; the human-readable address block only requires 5 or 9 digits.
  • Failure to properly sequester Residual mail can result in Seamless Acceptance Assessment Penalties.
IMPORTANT REMINDERS
  • Letters vs. Flats: Residual pricing is only flat-rated for Letters. Flats fall back to exact Single-Piece rates.
  • Mixed AADC is NOT Residual: A mailpiece with perfect CASS data but no local matches goes to Mixed AADC (Discounted), NOT Residual.
  • NCOA Impact: Move Update failures are the primary driver of mail dropping into the Residual category in an automated pipeline.
